## 2. Problem Depth

### Current Solutions

**Hotel side:** Almost entirely manual. Hotels receive 10-20 influencer pitches per day (Maldives properties). Only ~10% are worth considering. Marketing directors manually review Instagram profiles, check engagement rates, assess audience demographics, and negotiate terms over long email chains. Some use Evolusen, WIWT, or generic platforms like GRIN ($28K+/year), but no dominant hospitality-specific solution exists.

**Influencer side:** Cold-pitch emails in bulk. Out of 100 emails sent, ~40 get opened, ~15 respond, and only **1-2 result in collaborations** — a 1-2% success rate. The process involves finding contact info (often wrong), writing personalized pitches, following up multiple times, and negotiating terms.

### What Competitors Get Wrong

5. **No one owns "match quality."** A vegan wellness influencer shouldn't be matched with a hunting lodge. AI-powered matching on brand alignment, audience demographics, content style, and property type is wide open.

## 7. Reasons It Would Fail

1. **The "good enough" manual process wins.** 80% of hotels manage influencer relationships via email and spreadsheets. The pain is real but tolerable. If your platform doesn't deliver 10X better results, adoption stalls at early adopters.
2. **Booking attribution is technically unsolvable at the individual creator level.** Multi-device, multi-session booking journeys (Instagram → Google → OTA → direct) make it nearly impossible to attribute a specific booking to a specific influencer post. Without attribution, your core value proposition collapses.
3. **Marketplace leakage kills unit economics.** After the first successful match, hotels and influencers go direct. You become a dating app where successful matches leave the platform. Commission revenue evaporates.
4. **Sojern or a major platform enters the space.** Sojern has 10,000+ hotel customers and $120M+ in funding. If they add an influencer matching module, they win on distribution alone. Same risk from GRIN, Aspire, or CreatorIQ adding a hospitality vertical.
5. **Curation doesn't scale.** The entire value proposition rests on being selective. As you grow beyond 500-1,000 influencers, maintaining consistent quality becomes an operational nightmare. One bad influencer experience poisons a hotel's trust in the platform.
6. **Hotels have seasonal, lumpy demand.** Boutique hotels want influencers during shoulder seasons (to boost low occupancy), not peak season (when they're full). This creates massive supply/demand imbalance — all hotels want influencers in the same off-peak windows.
7. **Influencer marketing fatigue.** Consumer trust in influencer recommendations is declining (58% of consumers say they trust influencer content less than 2 years ago per some surveys). If the channel's effectiveness declines, hotels will shift budgets elsewhere.
8. **Cold start problem is brutal for two-sided marketplaces.** You need hotels to attract influencers and influencers to attract hotels. Without density in specific destinations, neither side gets value. Most marketplace startups die here.
9. **Hotel tech sales cycles are slow.** Boutique hotel GMs wear many hats and are not quick to adopt new software. Average hospitality SaaS sales cycle: 30-90 days. At $1,000/mo ACV, this is a tough grind.
10. **Retaliatory behavior from rejected influencers.** Exclusivity means rejecting creators. Rejected influencers have platforms and audiences. They can publicly criticize your platform, write negative reviews, and discourage others from joining.

## 8. Reasons It Would Succeed

1. **The market timing is perfect.** Influencer marketing in hospitality is growing 25-40% annually. Hotels are shifting budgets from OTAs to direct channels. Creator commerce is exploding. You're riding multiple compounding tailwinds simultaneously.
2. **No one owns the middle market.** Enterprise platforms (GRIN, CreatorIQ) cost $28K-$100K/year. Low-end options (Stayamo) are classifieds boards. There is literally no quality solution at $500-$2,000/mo for independent boutique hotels. This is a pricing gap you can own.
3. **Curation is a genuine differentiator that compounds.** Unlike open marketplaces where more users = more noise, curated networks become more valuable with scale. "I'm on [Platform]" becomes a status signal for influencers. "We only work with [Platform]-vetted creators" becomes a quality signal for hotels. This is the Raya/Soho House effect applied to B2B.
4. **If you crack attribution, you're untouchable.** Proprietary data on which creator-hotel pairings drive actual bookings is an unassailable moat. No competitor can replicate your historical match-to-booking dataset. Hotels will pay premium prices for proven ROI data.
5. **M&A market is red-hot.** Publicis spent $500M+ on influencer acquisitions in 18 months. Travel tech saw $10.8B in M&A in H1 2025. You sit at the intersection of two active acquisition categories. A platform bridging influencer marketing + hospitality tech is a natural bolt-on for multiple acquirer types.
6. **LTV:CAC ratio is exceptional.** Even conservatively modeled, hotel LTV:CAC exceeds 18:1. Influencer acquisition is nearly free (they want access to free hotel stays). The economics support aggressive growth spending.
7. **AI makes this possible now in a way it wasn't before.** LLMs can analyze creator content style, audience demographics, brand alignment, and property characteristics to produce match quality scores that would have required expensive human curation 3 years ago. This is the "why now" that justifies a new entrant.

### Smallest Version That Generates a Money Signal

**Concierge MVP:** Manually match 10 hotels with 20-30 influencers. You are the algorithm. Use a simple intake form (hotel submits property details, target audience, budget) and a curated influencer database (vetted profiles in a spreadsheet/Airtable). You make the matches, facilitate introductions, and track results via promo codes.

**What you're testing:** Will hotels pay for curated matches? Will influencers accept the matches? Do the matches drive bookings?

### Build vs No-Code Decision

**No-code for MVP.** Use:

- Airtable: hotel and influencer databases, match tracking
- Typeform: intake forms for both sides
- Zapier: workflow automation (notifications, email sequences)
- Stripe: payment processing for hotel subscriptions
- Simple landing page (Framer or Webflow)

Build custom only after validating with 20+ paying hotels.

### The Single Feature That Matters Most

**Match quality scoring.** The one thing that differentiates you from every competitor is the quality of the match between a specific influencer's audience and a specific hotel's target guest. Get this right (even manually at first) and everything else follows.

### Timeline

- **Week 1-2:** 20 discovery calls (10 hotels, 10 influencers). Build landing page. Set up Airtable.
- **Week 3-4:** Onboard first 5 hotels (free pilot). Manually source and vet 30 influencer profiles.
- **Month 2:** Facilitate first 5-10 matches. Track with promo codes. Collect feedback obsessively.
- **Month 3:** If matches drive bookings → charge hotel #6-10. First revenue. If not → pivot matching criteria or target segment.

| **OVERALL**          | **7.1**      |                                                                                                                                                                                                     |


### Verdict: **CAUTION — Leaning GO**

This is a validated market with real pain, strong unit economics, and a clear competitive gap. The timing is excellent — influencer marketing in hospitality is growing 25-40% annually, AI makes matching feasible at scale, and M&A activity is at historic highs.

**What holds it back from a clean GO:**

The two existential risks are (1) **booking attribution** — if you can't prove influencer content drives hotel bookings, your value proposition is just "better matchmaking," which is harder to defend and monetize, and (2) **marketplace leakage** — hotels and influencers going direct after the first match could kill your commission revenue. These are solvable problems but they're not trivially solvable, and your entire business model depends on getting at least one of them right.

**The path forward is clear:** Run a 60-day concierge MVP with 5-10 hotels and 20-30 influencers. Manually match. Track with promo codes. If you can prove even 3 bookings attributable to your matches, you have the evidence to charge hotels and raise a pre-seed round.

### The Single Thing That Would Change This Verdict

**Proof that your matches drive measurable hotel bookings at a higher rate than the hotel's existing influencer partnerships.** One case study showing "Hotel X got 15 bookings ($12,000 revenue) from a creator we matched them with, versus 2 bookings from their last 3 self-sourced influencer partnerships" would upgrade this to a hard GO and make the business fundable.

### What Evidence in Week 1 Would Upgrade to GO?

- 5+ hotel marketing directors agree to a free pilot after a single cold message (signals desperate pain)
- 3+ hotels independently say "I would pay $500-$1,000/mo for this today if it existed" in discovery calls
- A hotel tech platform (Cloudbeds, Mews) or trade association (BLLA) expresses interest in partnership or distribution deal
- You identify a clear, replicable way to attribute bookings to influencer content (even if imperfect — promo codes + UTM links may be enough to start)
