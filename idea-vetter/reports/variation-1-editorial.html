The Problem

Hotels waste thousands on influencer partnerships that don't convert. Influencers waste time cold-pitching with a 1-2% success rate.

Both sides lack a reliable matching mechanism. Hotels receive 10-20 influencer pitches per day at desirable properties, with only ~10% worth considering. Marketing directors manually review Instagram profiles, check engagement rates, and negotiate over long email chains.

The Solution

A curated, exclusive two-sided marketplace that carefully matches vetted travel influencers with boutique/luxury hotels based on audience-property fit. Performance-based compensation: free stays + commission on bookings generated.

07
Reasons It Would Fail
  1. The "good enough" manual process wins
    80% of hotels use spreadsheets & email. Pain is real but tolerable.
  2. Booking attribution is technically unsolvable
    Multi-device, multi-session journeys make attribution nearly impossible at the individual creator level.
  3. Marketplace leakage kills unit economics
    Hotels and influencers go direct after the first match. You become a dating app where success means churn.
  4. Sojern or a major platform enters
    10K+ hotel customers and $120M+ in funding. They win on distribution alone.
  5. Curation doesn't scale
    Maintaining quality beyond 500-1,000 influencers becomes an operational nightmare.
08
Reasons It Would Succeed
  1. Perfect market timing
    Influencer marketing in hospitality growing 25-40% annually. Multiple compounding tailwinds.
  2. No one owns the middle market
    Enterprise = $28K-$100K/yr. Low-end = classifieds. Zero quality solutions at $500-$2K/mo.
  3. Curation compounds as a differentiator
    The Raya/Soho House effect. "I'm on [Platform]" becomes a status signal.
  4. Attribution = unassailable moat
    If you crack it, no competitor can replicate your historical match-to-booking dataset.
  5. M&A market is red-hot
    You sit at the intersection of two active acquisition categories. Multiple exit paths.
